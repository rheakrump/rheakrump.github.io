Projects

The following are three examples of projects in which I utilized skills that will be beneficial to my future career. The projects vary; two are more academic, and the last one began as an assignment and became a volunteer project by the time of its completion.

Every year, the Veterans of Foreign Wars hosts an essay competition for high school students. The prompts vary, but always touch on Americanism and patriotism. My junior and senior year of high school, I placed second at state in this contest. Junior year, the prompt was “Is this the country the founding fathers envisioned?” and senior year, the prompt was, “Where do we go from here?”

In a college course about British Romanticism, I chose to complete a creative final. To do so, I composed an imitation poem, based on “Indian Woman’s Death Song” by Felicia Hemans. I used the themes and poetic structure of Hemans’ work, but my source material was entirely different. I based this poem on Saint Gianna Beretta Molla’s life story.

My senior year of high school, I had the opportunity to create a program to benefit people in my community. To do this, a friend and I worked together to make “Summer Fun Packages” for lower income families. The packages included pool passes, restaurant gift cards, and summer toys. This form of writing is essential because all public policy and programs begin as an idea, then that idea must be effectively communicated in order to go anywhere.
